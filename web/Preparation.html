Preparation

Warnings and Alerts

There are many ways to receive alerts. One way to receive alerts and recognize warnings are to check the home page map. If you see that a pinpoint near your location is starting to rise then begin planning evacuation and swiftly leave before the wildfire spreads too far.

Emergency Plan

Be sure to have an emergency plan for evcauating wildfires. Recognize evacuation zones that are close to your home and practice with your household and pets to make sure they will be able to know where to go. Follow instrucitons from local authorities as they will provide the latest recommendations and information based on how threatning the wildfire is to your community. Lastly prepare and review important documents (ID, license) in the event that your home is ruined by the fire.

Strengthen Your Home

When buidling or renovating your home near common wildfire places, make sure to use fire-resistant materials. Also find an outdoor water source with a hose that can reach any part of your property. Next create a fire-resistant zone acround your home that is free of any flammable materials for at least 30 feet. Lastly designate a room that can be closed off to outside air so that room is not polluted by outdoor smoky condiitons.

Gather Supplies

In the event of an emergency where the wildfire is rapidly heading to your home, have enough supplies for your family. Include a first aid kit in your car or bag. By being prepared, your family avoids any unncessary minor medical issues which eases the burden of urgent care centers. Additionally, you are able to decrease response time toward such emergencies. Be careful when using any flammable or combusitble products. If able to, store n95 maks to protect your fmaily from the smoke. Lastly, keep your cell phone charged as they will be important for information and mapping.
