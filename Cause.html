Causes of Wildfires

Humans

In a 2017 study looking at 1.6 million wildfires, people caused at least 60% of the wildfires in California. Common sources of human-caused wildfires include:

  • Debris or yard waste fires
  • Arson
  • Camping Equipment(Ones that can create sparks)
  • Camp Fires
  • Fireworks

Lightning

The most common natural cause of wildfires is lighitng. For example in the Rocky Mountains and Southwest, lighting caused most of the wildfires.They are known to cause wildfire in drier and more remote areas.

Societal Infrastructure

Another common cause of wildfires are electrical power. When power lines fall or break, the cords can create sparks which cause forest fires. Power lines falling are common as it only takes a branch falling to create a fire. Eletrical power causes nearly 10% of wildfires which is around 400 fires per year in California.

Droughts

Droughts can cause wildfires as during these low precipitaion times, grass and trees can dry out and become more flammable. As they become more flammable, they also become fuels for wildfires. As such, droughts can increase the probability of ignition of wildfires and the rate at which these fires can spread.
