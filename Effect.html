Effects

Air Pollution

Wildfires are dangerous as they can increase air pollution. This is done by killing plants which purify the air and releasing greenhouse gasses into the atmosphere. Plants are very important to air quality as they take in carbon dioxide and turn into oxygen which we can breath. However by killing these plants, wildfires reduce the amount of oxygen we can breathe and increase the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere which is poisonous to humans.

Health

Wildfires impact the health of many across the region and near wildfire locations. On average 17 firefighters die per year in wildfire incidients. Wildfires also impact those with allergies and respiratory disorders by causing intesnse breathing discomfort.

Economic Losses

Wildfires are huge econmic losses as many resources are required to put out these fires. To imagine the cost, an army of firefighters are needed to be deployed to extinguish fires, airplanes are deployed to drop large amounts of water, and fleets of firetrucks and command centers are used to move firefighters. Not only do wildfires cost a lot to put out, many civilians are affected by wildfires. In 2020, fires destoryed over 10,000 sturcutres which costed over $12 Billion in damages.

Damage Ecosystems

Wildfires are dangerous to ecosystems as they destory the habitats of many species and intricate relationship between these animals. In simpler terms, wildfires destroy habitats and apdatable land for various animals and plants which are the foundation to a greater ecosystems. As such, by killing off foundational animals and plants, they subsequently affect the whole ecosystem forcing animals out of regions or causing extinction of species.
